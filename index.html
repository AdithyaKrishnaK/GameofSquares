Instructions

Your aim is to guide the yellow square to the target by programming the path of the yellow square. The target is invisible but you will be be given either column or row of the target, maximum number of commands you can use and the minimum number of steps you can walk in the bottom of the editor. Note that the number representing first row and first column is 0. If you succeed, in the next level you will have to navigate to the target through the purple square. You will be be given either column or row of the target, maximum number of commands you can use and the minimum number of steps you can walk in the bottom of the editor. You can click on the compass to get your bearing and if you click it again the compass will be hidden. The first number in the bottom of the editor is the maximum number of commands you can use. The second number is the minimum number of steps you have to walk. Here are the commands that you can use -

walk(20);
- This means that the yellow square will move 20 steps foreward.

turn(N);
- This means that the yellow square will move foreward the next time it moves. This is default direction.

turn(E);
- This means that the yellow square will move towards right the next time it moves.

turn(S);
- This means that the yellow square will move towards backwards the next time it moves.

turn(W);
- This means that the yellow square will move towards left the next time it moves.

end()
- This marks the end the program.

For example:

walk(3);
turn(E);
walk(4);
end()

This means that the yellow square will move 3 blocks foreward and then turns right walk 4 blocks and terminates the program. It's your turn now.
For help click the help icon at the bottom of the editor
